# Natural Gas Price Volatility Analysis

## Overview
Analysis of 20+ years of Henry Hub natural gas spot price data to identify
price trends, seasonal volatility patterns, and trading risk implications.

## Business Problem
Natural gas price volatility creates significant market exposure for trading
desks, risk managers, and middle office functions. This analysis provides
data-driven visibility into when and why prices spike — supporting daily
risk reporting and counterparty exposure monitoring.

## Tools Used
- **SQL** — data extraction, cleaning, and transformation
- **Excel** — pivot table analysis and trend modeling
- **Tableau** — interactive dashboard (link below)

## Data Source
- U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
- Henry Hub Natural Gas Spot Price
- Coverage: 2000–2024 (20+ years, 6,000+ daily observations)

## Key Findings
- Winter months consistently show the highest price volatility
- Peak monthly price swings exceeded 40% during supply disruptions
- 2005 and 2022 recorded the highest volatility years on record
- Rolling 30-day volatility is a reliable indicator of elevated trading risk
